# Essence Economy

## Generation

Domain expression across the universe generates Essence per Domain per tick. The amount generated is a function of **intensity** — how strongly a Domain is expressed — not merely presence. Increasing Domain expression is therefore the central lever of the game's economy.

## Distribution

Luminaries claim the majority of Essence generated by their Domains. The Demiurge designates **three Domains** from which they personally claim Essence. Any Domain not claimed by the Demiurge flows almost entirely to whichever Luminary holds it.

The three-Domain claim is a strategic commitment: the Demiurge has incentive to build infrastructure (POIs, Imāgō pressure, direct interventions) around their claimed Domains, because those are the only ones where increased intensity pays dividends back to them.

## The Peter Principle

Increasing Domain expression enriches Luminaries and raises their disposition toward the Demiurge — but also raises their **baseline expectations** for future ticks. A Demiurge who consistently maximizes output is negotiating their own performance bar upward. Being too competent is a liability.

This creates pressure to manage output deliberately: keep certain Domains modestly expressed to avoid committing to expectations you cannot sustain, while investing in the three claimed Domains to fund your own agenda.

## Underreal Harvesting

The Demiurge may take an action to harvest Essence directly from the Underreal. This bypasses the Domain expression economy entirely — it does not require building up regional expression or waiting for tick generation.

- Almost universally disapproved of by Luminaries.
- Carries additional dangers (see `underreal.md`).
- Useful when Essence-starved, especially to fund an Imāgō action before the normal tick economy can supply it.

The Underreal as an off-books income source lets the Demiurge operate outside Luminary oversight — which is precisely why Luminaries object to it.

## Domain-Pushing Monoliths

The Demiurge can create a monolith POI that radiates Domain expression outward from its placement coordinate, gradually shifting the history-paced layer of regional character (see `geography.md`).

### Upkeep

Monoliths require ongoing Essence to function. If the Demiurge cannot or chooses not to pay the upkeep cost, the monolith enters a **dormant** state: still physically present on the map, still a POI, but radiating nothing. A dormant monolith may still be discovered and interpreted by nearby populations. Reactivating a dormant monolith produces a brief spike of Domain expression before settling into steady-state.

### Placement and Footprint

The **overtness** of creating a monolith is modulated by context:

- Placement in an uninhabited wilderness with no sapient witnesses substantially reduces the divine footprint of the action.
- Placement near a population center is faster to influence but leaves a much larger footprint.
- A population's existing beliefs affect how the monolith is interpreted: a shamanistic culture expecting divine signs may read it as "subtle" even if it is technically a physical artifact; a highly rationalist culture would find it alarming.

### Self-financing loop

A monolith pushing a Domain the Demiurge has claimed will generate more Essence from that Domain — partially offsetting its own upkeep cost. A monolith pushing a Luminary's Domain enriches the Luminary and may raise expectations without benefiting the Demiurge.

### Luminary constraints

Some Luminaries/pantheons categorically prohibit physical artifacts; others set threshold-based limits on total overt footprint per tick. Domain-specific rules are also possible (e.g., a Luminary of Silence permitting Void-expression monoliths but penalizing Passion-expression ones).
